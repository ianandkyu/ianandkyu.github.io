Forward

Spring 2019
Pratt Institute


This is a record of our time.


During our years at Pratt we’ve developed a sort of comradery. We met during our sophomore year and quickly developed a relationship based on a mutual admiration of each other’s work and a shared feeling of being out of place.
Since then we shared our time together as if we were two travellers on a train that happened to chance on a good conversation. We shared with each other our thoughts and ideas and we’d like to think that both of us have grown through this process.
But as with all things we find ourselves now at the end of our journey. And with that we’ve begun to wonder whether there is any way we could keep the time we had as a record both for us and others like us who find themselves in similar situations with similar thoughts.
These thoughts and ideas are neither profound nor unique in anyway, but still they are our own and we cherish them as a part of our own spirit.

Initial Statements

Ian Griffin

For senior project, it would be valuable for me to document or somehow make use of the conversations that Kyu and I have. Not only for us, to document a bond, but also because I think others could benefit from hearing our conversations. I know Kyu from sophomore year at Pratt when we both took Typography I. I remember how both of us made 18X24” posters about ourselves at the end of the semester. Kyu used Adobe Garamond and I used Linotype Univers. I think at a certain point that year, Kyu invited me over to his place and we talked about what it’s all about, whatever that is. A few years pass by.
Eventually, Kyu went to the army. Ian took some time off. We both returned to school, and reconnected.
Last year, Kyu prints from his labelmaker “but we still continue” and gives it to me, sometime last semester, which I now have on my phone.
Over the Summer of 2018, I lived with Kyu in a residence hall at Pratt Institute (BFA) Kyu was working at the school in Creative Service as a Student Designer doing internal branding for Pratt, while I was at Wieden+Kennedy. I feel like this was a really formative period for both of us, having time off to relax as well as working and experimenting. The best were the conversations, and from just living and the human experience, we began to talk about art and design.
I’ll be honest, I fetishize and collect books, but I rarely have the time. There’s always more time to read them all to completion later. This stems from somewhere. At a very young age, I loved reading so much that I would buy thick books at a fairly early age, usually fairytales or fantasy because of the dragon illustrations on the cover. At a certain point, I became somewhat overwhelmed. My imagination was still untethered and there was so much to read, but also games to play and Legos to build. Maybe I’m just lazy, but I almost abandoned reading because of this. I liked cover illustrations, though. Maybe this is why I got into my field of practice. Although this happened to me, I can’t say for certain whether this distance from reading aided or inhibited my creativity, concentration, or rigor.
Regardless, back in my dorm room, I show Kyu a cool book I bought sometime in 2017 after reading a good portion at the Strand. The book was Hippie Modernism published by the Walker Art Center. I think he appreciated this exchange because he got very enthused by the section on ONYX. ONYX blew his mind.
In a collaboration, the participants come together to form something. There is compromise, or overlap inbetween the two minds, their perspectives and subjectivites, on the way or how. I wonder if this can be bypassed, or if what Ron Williams of ONYX calls an anticollective may occur. He says in an interview, “We were in favor of a diversity of ideas rather than a singular idea.” Perhaps our conversations might be this, and if so, then there must be a visual analog as well. No certain way or how, but room for all.
I feel like that Summer was really positive for Kyu and Me. We hung out all the time and I feel like we both explored our own opinions and thoughts on making during this time through bouncing ideas. I think we created a really positive space for creative exploration, even if that just meant talking through the ideas around us, digging up the Earth, and making room for new.
Is this peace?
But the anti-collective is still based on some grounds of similarity. Over that summer, Kyu worked on the Prattfolio cover for the “Empathy” issue. This idea has appeared in his work before, as I return to this idea of community in my work, such as the parties I held and posters I’ve made for them . Somehow, it feels that we are searching for some understanding within one another, for each of our own reasons. For Senior Project, Kyu and I are going to print words. These words will be bound in concept and design, meaning the form reflects the content. These words will come from conversations we have over the next coming months. I would like for the forms to be reproducible so that others who are interested can learn from our conversations in the same way I have learned from him. Because of this, we can think of this as a book, perhaps post-artifact and/or post-digital.
This would be online using a website called GitHub so that even the architecture of the website can be pulled apart and used by others as template for their own content for free. However, I would also like to explore what happens when these words take another form, perhaps as a book, whose role and character has dramatically shifted since the dawn of the internet.
This would be a great opportunity to speak about the scenarios we find ourselves in as graphic designers, artists, and producers, and discover visual forms that combine the voices of Kyu and I.
If it were a GitHub, it should be a format that is styled easily for conversations. I was thinking <h1> could be my words and <h2> could be Kyu’s words, with styled containers. Then, <header> would be !?, which we possibly float behind the content. Kyu came up to me one day and said how ! is an i turned upside down, and his name was Kyu, like Q. However, it is important to note that neither Kyu nor I are designated to being the question or the exclamation, the voice, the call.This could dictate <spans>for the Q or Kyu: or IG: or !ᵷ or ?yu! or whatever it is. Kyu will be doing the transcribing of our recordings. The format of the transcriptions is his to choose, completely in his hands. However, maybe it’s not about a transcription or back and forth. It could be a quote on a poster if we really boil down what we’re thinking into one idea. I’m open to format.
I was thinking we could give it a structure so you could see multiple topics at once, say, if you break your pages up into conversations or days or quotes or however you are choosing to organize thoughts. We could use flexbox in CSS to arrange a grid of items, kind of like a chart. Each box will have a stroke and ample padding around the text which is centered in the box. Perhaps in Javascript we can find a way to randomize which box gets which added .class value, changing their padding to give variation, making it more like tiles than a grid. Using flexbox is kind of like creating rows boxes instead of columns. Their size and padding could also be dynamic depending on which box your cursor is hovered over, changing the size of other boxes potentially. For the documents themselves, images could float freely. Text set in <p> with <h1> and <h2>. Clicking on the ?! takes you back. The navigation could also be one half of the screen and the content could be the other half. The half and half mechanism seems to be proper, or could be dynamic as well. The only problem with half and half is that I want it to be 1 + 1.
When Kyu presented this senior project idea to me, the first thing I said to him was about fireflies. “You want to catch them, but you don’t want to crush them. How will we capture the thoughts with ease?” Kyu thought we could just record our conversations. I began recording on my cellphone. It actually felt pretty natural. I was on board.

Kyu Ho Lee

How can I help people thousands of miles away if I can’t help the people around me. The people that sit right next to me.
What do I call myself. The young man who hates the systems so much that everything is based on this hate. I realize that perhaps a voice of hope is better. That I can shift the views of people. That it’s not just about how the world is out to get us. But how we can survive in it. So I created my first senior thesis based on that. How can we create new culture in what is deemed as societal escape. Maybe it will work. But I look to the people around me and I see that the anxieties I have, this overwhelming feeling that we will never be good enough and that even if we can that we will have to sacrifice everything for it. In a world that seeks to commoditize everything. We live as graphic designers, artists who play with this system. We stretch the definition of design to try and fit our hearts and souls in the picture, to show that even though this is an ad it was still made by a human and that human cares so much more than having the product marketed. That there is still a common basis in trying to live a life that is true.
For this last semester I want to care. I want to care about graphic design and I want to care about the people around me. I think for me helping has always been a part of who I am. To try and help people navigate this world as I try myself. We’ve begun to have conversations. We walk to the park. We trip acid and walk the streets of Brooklyn. The topic is always the same. We talk about art, love, and our place in the world. And how we can put something genuine into what we do. Something that doesn’t come from a place of ego, because we hate it when it does. But something that comes from a place of love. A love for humanity, the arts, and most importantly beauty.
How can we be honest with what we find beautiful. As graphic designers we find ourselves having to compromise these things. The client loves a specific style, the client wants the standard. The brand dominates us. Consumerism dominates us. But the systems are there regardless. I think starting to change it comes not with a radical protest against it. I think of David Foster Wallace and his essay on American TV. The things that protest the system becomes the larger tyrants themselves, it is the nature of commodification. So then what?
I think compromise is something we can never get away from. But in the end isn’t life one big compromise? We’re born here not because we want to. We just are. And we are expected to live the life and abide by society’s standards. We can’t do whatever we want. Instead we can do what we can. What we can to make the world a little bit better. Somewhat bearable and somewhat true. Grasping on to that is what helps us move. To wake up another day. This piece is an anti-collaboration, an anti-collective. We realize that our ways our different, but they come from the same place. A place of being human. We celebrate these differences because they are the definition of human. This piece is a conversation between two radically different individuals as they grapple with the concept of the soul in graphic design. A publication of the culmination of our four years here at Pratt. We hope that people will read it and be inspired to live a life true to their own being.
This piece is memorabilia. For us to remember our time here and to never forget the thoughts that we’ve had here. That we will never forget to be true to our work. That we will never forget each other.
This piece is an experiment to discover the different ways we can be true not only to our work but to ourselves.
This piece is our love for the world and our hope that things can be better.
